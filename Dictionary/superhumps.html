Superhumps

Superhumps are periodic variations in the light curves of SU UMa stars observed during the superoutbursts of these systems. The period of superhumps are slighlty longer than the orbital period of the binary. Their amplitudes are ~ 0.2 - 0.7 magnitudes and decrease at a rate of about 0.02 - 0.05 magnitudes / day. Superhumps are due to tidal effects in the outer regions of the accretion disk, which create a highly eccentric outer ring (Smak 2013).
