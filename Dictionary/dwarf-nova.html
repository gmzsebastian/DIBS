Dwarf Nova

Dwarf novae are a class of cataclysmic variables stars that show episodic outbursts lasting from days to weeks in which the brightness of the soure increases by ~ 2 - 8 magnitudes. These outbursts have recurrence times of weeks to years. They are produced by instabilities that are triggered by build up of matter in the accretion disk. Dwarf novae are not to be confused with recurrent novae or classical novae, which also show outbursts, but these are caused by build up of matter on the surface of the white dwarf (Coppejans et al. 2016).

There are three classes of dwarf novae: U Gem stars show similar outbursts throughout their life, SU UMa stars have superoutbursts next to normal outbursts, and Z Cam stars show outbursts interrupted by irregular periods of constant brightness. There are about 650 dwarf novae known to date (Otulakowska-Hypka et al. 2016).
