High Mass X-ray Binary (HMXB)

A high mass X-ray binary is a sub-class of X-ray binaries. Theses systems are made of either a neutron star or a black hole accreting matter from a high mass donor star. By definition, the donor star is more massive than the accretor, which is typically an O or B type star with a mass greater than about 8M⊙. Accretion in HMXBs usually happens via winds from the massive donor, or a focused wind onto the accretor. HMXBs have periods ranging from 2 ~ 260 days. And we know of ~ 116 HMXBs in our galaxy (Liu et al. 2006). The canonical example of a HMXB is the very well studied X-ray binary, Cygnus X-1.
