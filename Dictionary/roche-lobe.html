Roche Lobe

A Roche Lobe is a tear-drop shaped boundary region around a star in a binary system. Anything in the area outside of the Roche lobe of a star can orbit or escape the star, while anything that lies inside the Roche lobe is gravitationally bound and will eventually collide with the star. The lines in the figure below represent equipotential lines. Accretion in low mass X-ray binaries happens via Roche lobe overflow.
