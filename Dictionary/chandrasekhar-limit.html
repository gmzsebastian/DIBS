Chandrasekhar Limit

The Chandrasekhar limit is the maximum theoretical mass of a white dwarf, calculated to be = 1.38M⊙. Anything with a mass above that would collapse into a neutron star. The limit is named after Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar, who worked on improving this calculation in 1931 (Chandrasekhar 1931)
