Wolf-Rayet Star (WR)

Wolf-Rayet stars are a class of stars with very strong winds. These stars are extremely hot, from 30 - 200 thousand degrees Kelvin, and have depleated their hydrogen envelope. The most massive stars known are Wolf-Rayet stars. These stars have also been suggested to be the progenitors of Type Ib/Ic supernovae (Yoon et al. 2017).
