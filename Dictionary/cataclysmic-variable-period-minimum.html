Cataclysmic Variable Period Minimum

The orbital period of cataclysmic variables is well described by the equation P \propto \sqrt\frac{R^3_2}{M_2} , where R_2 and where M_2 are the radius and mass of the secondary star, the one donating mass. The histogram of observed periods of cataclysmic variables has a sharp cutoff at a period of ~ 78 minutes. There are no systems with periods below 73 minutes, and only 12 systems with periods below 83 minutes (King 2002).

The theoretical reason behind the CV period minimum is explained as follows: As the mass of the donor is reduced due to accretion onto the white dwarf, the radius of the secondary decreases and therefore the period of the system must decrease (see the equation above). But when the mass of the donor falls below about 0.1M⊙ the radius of the star does not decrease as efficiently as its mass, the mass of the donor falls faster than R_2^3 , and the period must therefore start to increase.
