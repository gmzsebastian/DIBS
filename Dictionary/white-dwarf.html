White Dwarf

White dwarfs are the remnant cores left behind after a low mass star ends its life. Stars burn fuel throught their life, when they run out of fuel they expell part of their mass into a planetary nebula, but their core remains gravitationally bound in the form of a white dwarf. After our Sun ends its life, it will leave behind a white dwarf. They are very dense objects with radii similar to the radius of the Earth, but the typical mass of a white dwarf is about 0.6M⊙. White dwarfs are not as dense as neutron stars. The maximum mass a white dwarf can have is the Chandrasekhar mass = 1.39M⊙. White dwarfs have been observed to have masses anywhere between 0.17 - 1.35M⊙ (Madej et al. 2004). White dwarfs are made of degenerate matter, which means that the more massive ones are actually smaller.
