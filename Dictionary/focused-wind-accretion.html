Focused Wind Accretion

Isolated stars have isotropic winds, emitting equally in all directions. But when a star is in a binary system and has a companion such as in an X-ray binary, the stellar wind is not necessarily isotrpic, but can be strongly targeted towards the compact object. This increase in the flow of matter towards the compact object makes accretion more efficient (Hanke et al. 2009).
