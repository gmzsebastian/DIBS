Low Mass X-ray Binary (LMXB)

A low mass X-ray binary is an X-ray binary where the donor star is by definition less massive than the accretor, the donor is usually less than ~ 2M⊙. The accretor can be either a black hole or a neutron star. Accretion in LMXBs usually happens via Roche lobe overflow. The orbital periods of these systems are relatively short, in between 3 - 13 hours (Tetarenko et al. 2016).
