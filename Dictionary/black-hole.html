Black Hole

A black hole is the remnant left behind after a very massive star dies. When a star dies its core can collapse to a very small radius, if the radius is smaller than the Schwarzschild radius, then it becomes a black hole. The most notable aspect of black holes is that nothing that crosses this radis can even come back, not even light. The latest estimate predicts that there are about 104 - 108 stellar mass black holes in high mass X-ray binaries in our galaxy (Tetarenko et al. 2016). Stellar mass black holes are the end states of the most massive stars, unlike supermassive black holes, which have masses greater than about 105M⊙ and live in the centers of galaxies.
