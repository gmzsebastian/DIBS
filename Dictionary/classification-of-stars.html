Classification of Stars

Stars are classified by their color, and since stars radiate as blackbodies, their color is directly related to their temperature. The classification scheme goes as O, B, A, F, G, K, M in decreasing order of temperature. O stars are the hottest and bluest, and M stars are the coldest and redest. Each letter is referred to as a "spectral type", which can be further subclassified into 9 divisions, i.e. O9, O8, O7,... in decreasing order of temperature, sometimes non-integer classifications are used (i.e. O8.5 is between O9 and O8).

Stars can also be classified based on their luminosity. A star spends most of its life as a main sequence star, then as it evolves it expands to become a giant, then a supergiant, and so on. The luminosity classes of stars in increasing order of luminosity is listed below:

  • VII: Brown dwarfs
  • VI: Red dwarfs
  • V: Main sequence star
  • IV: Subgiants
  • III: Giants
  • II: Giants
  • Ib: Bright giants
  • Ia: Supergiants
  • 0: Hypergiants
