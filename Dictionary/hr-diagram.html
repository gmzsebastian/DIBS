HR Diagram

The Hertzsprungâ€“Russell diagram (HR Diagram) is a graph that relates the temperature of a group of stars, to their luminosity. The horizontal axis shows the temperature of the stars from greatest to lowest, reversed from a traditional plot. The vertical axis shows the luminosity of the stars. Since the magnitude of a star is related to its luminosity, and the color of a star is related to its temperature, and HR diagram might replace luminosity with magnitude, or color with temperature.

One can usually see a long diagonal line of stars going from top left to bottom right of the HR diagram, that is known as the main sequence band, populated by stars known as main sequence stars. Stars live most of their life in the main sequence, and then transition onto the horizontal branch, red giant branch, and asymptotic giant branch. Which are the group of stars in the upper right section of the diagram. The small group of stars on the lower left corner are white dwarf.
