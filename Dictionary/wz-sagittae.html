WZ Sagittae (WZ Sge)

WZ Sge is a dwarf nova that was discovered in 1981 after it had an outburst in 1979 (Patterson et al. 1981). WZ Sge is a white dwarf star accreting via Roche lobe overflow from a low mass companion. It has a long outburst recurrence time of ~ 30 days, and a large outburst amplitude of ~ 7 magnitudes. It has undergone outbursts in 1931, 1946, 1978, and 2001 (Steeghs et al. 2007). This system is associated with cataclysmic variables near the period minimum. The parameters of the system are listed on the table below.

WZ Sge-type stars are now a category of stars simliar to WZ Sge. They are a subtype of SU UMa-type dwarf novae. There are about 100 WZ Sge-type systems known, 63% of them show superhumps with an amplitude larger than 0.02mag. These objects show large outburst amplitudes of ~ 8 magnitudes, slow decline from outbursts, and long intervals in between outbursts. They have typical orbital periods of ~ 1.4 hours (Kato 2015).
