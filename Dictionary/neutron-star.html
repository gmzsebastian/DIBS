Neutron Star

A neutron star is a very dense compact object left behind after the death of a massive star in a supernova explosion. A low mass star, or a star like our Sun, will leave behind a white dwarf after it exhausts its fuel. A very massive star with a mass greater than ~ 8M⊙ will leave behind a neutron star. And the most massive stars greater than ~ 14M⊙ will leave behind black hole remnants (Sukhbold et al. 2016). Neutron stars have masses of about 1 ~ 2 solar masses and radii of ~ 6 - 15R⊙, the equation of state, or the relation between the mass and radii of neutron stars is subject to big debate right now (Ã–zel & Freire 2016). Neutron stars are made of degenerate matter, meaning that the more massive stars are smaller, and they are supported by neutron degeneracy pressure, which prevents them from collapsing into a black hole.
